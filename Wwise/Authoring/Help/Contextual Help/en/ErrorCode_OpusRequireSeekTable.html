Seek table required to seek in Opus sources. Please update conversion settings.

The sound engine could not seek in an Opus-encoded source. The Opus codec is a variable bit rate codec; therefore, to seek to a specific position, you need a seek table.

Probable causes:

  • An Event with the Seek Action was posted for an Opus-encoded sound without a seek table.

  • A call to AK::SoundEngine::SeekOnEvent on an Opus- encoded sound without a seek table.

  • For an Opus-encoded sound without a seek table, its Virtual Voice behavior (in Advanced Settings) is set to Play from elapsed time.

  • Your sound is shorter than the seek table granularity.

Recommended resolution steps:

  • Add a seek table in the Conversion Settings of the sound.

    Caution

    The adding of a seek table is almost always the solution to this problem. Be very cautious in applying the other solutions listed below, especially in changing the Virtual Voice settings.

  • Change the codec type in the Conversion Settings of the sound.

  • Change the Virtual Voice behavior in the Advanced Settings of the sound to something other than Play from elapsed time.

  • Set the seek table granularity to a lower number of frames, or choose a ShareSet with a small frame size.

  • Avoid seeking on this sound.
